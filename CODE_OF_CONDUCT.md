# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

**We encourage:**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Helping others learn and grow
- Sharing knowledge openly
- Celebrating contributions and achievements

### Unacceptable Behaviors

**We do not tolerate:**
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission
- Violent threats or language directed against another person
- Discriminatory jokes or language
- Intentional misgendering or use of dead names
- Sharing private conversations without permission
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement

### Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Primary Contact:**
- Email: conduct@educhain.io
- Response time: Within 24 hours

**Alternative Contacts:**
- GitHub: Report directly to repository maintainers
- Discord: Message moderators (coming soon)

### What to Include in Your Report

Please include:
- Your contact information (optional)
- The nature of the incident
- Date and time of the incident
- Individuals involved
- Any witnesses
- Screenshots or logs if available
- Desired resolution

### Investigation Process

1. **Immediate Response**: Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: Review all available information
3. **Action**: Determine appropriate response
4. **Resolution**: Communicate outcome to involved parties
5. **Follow-up**: Monitor for continued issues

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior may result in:
- **Warning**: Private or public warning
- **Temporary Suspension**: Limited access for specified period
- **Permanent Ban**: Removal from all community spaces
- **Legal Action**: For serious violations

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all community spaces including:
- GitHub repositories and organizations
- Issue trackers and pull requests
- Discussions and comments
- Social media channels
- Community events (online and in-person)
- Private communications related to the project

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Style

**Do:**
- Be patient and respectful
- Assume good faith
- Use clear and concise language
- Ask for clarification when needed
- Provide constructive feedback

**Don't:**
- Use aggressive or demanding language
- Make assumptions about others' knowledge
- Ignore questions or dismiss concerns
- Engage in arguments or debates

### Technical Discussions

**Best Practices:**
- Focus on technical merit, not personal preferences
- Provide evidence and reasoning for suggestions
- Be open to alternative approaches
- Acknowledge when you don't know something
- Help others understand complex topics

### Code Reviews

**Reviewers Should:**
- Be constructive and helpful
- Explain the reasoning behind suggestions
- Focus on the code, not the person
- Acknowledge good work and improvements
- Offer solutions, not just problems

**Authors Should:**
- Be open to feedback and suggestions
- Ask for clarification if needed
- Explain design decisions when helpful
- Thank reviewers for their time
- Learn from the feedback provided

## Diversity and Inclusion

### Commitment to Diversity

We are committed to creating an inclusive environment where:
- All voices are heard and valued
- Different perspectives are welcomed
- Everyone feels safe to contribute
- Barriers to participation are identified and removed

### Accessibility

We strive to make our community accessible by:
- Using clear and simple language
- Providing alternative formats when needed
- Being mindful of time zones and scheduling
- Accommodating different communication styles
- Ensuring digital accessibility

## Conflict Resolution

### Disagreements

Healthy disagreements are normal and expected. When they occur:
- Focus on the issue, not the person
- Listen to understand different perspectives
- Look for common ground and compromise
- Seek mediation if needed
- Maintain professional conduct throughout

### Escalation

If you're unable to resolve a conflict:
1. Try direct communication first
2. Involve a community moderator
3. Contact the conduct committee
4. Request formal mediation

## Recognition and Appreciation

### Celebrating Contributions

We recognize and celebrate:
- Code contributions and improvements
- Documentation and tutorials
- Community support and mentorship
- Bug reports and feedback
- Translation and localization efforts
- Design and accessibility improvements

### Ways to Show Appreciation
- Say thank you publicly and privately
- Highlight contributions in communications
- Invite contributors to special events

- Share success stories and case studies

### Educational Resources

We provide resources to help community members:
- Learn about blockchain technology
- Improve technical skills
- Understand community guidelines
- Develop leadership abilities
- Practice inclusive communication

### Mentorship Programs

We encourage mentorship through:
- Pairing experienced members with newcomers
- Providing guidance on contribution processes
- Offering technical and career advice
- Creating safe learning environments

## Enforcement Examples

### Examples of Acceptable Behavior

✅ A contributor uses respectful language and provides constructive feedback on a pull request

✅ A community member helps answer questions in discussions and points newcomers to helpful resources

✅ A maintainer explains technical decisions clearly and listens to alternative approaches

✅ Someone apologizes when they make a mistake and works to make things right

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

❌ Using sexualized language or making unwelcome advances

❌ Posting harassing or discriminatory content

❌ Sharing private information without permission

❌ Making threats or engaging in personal attacks

❌ Continuing unwanted behavior after being asked to stop
